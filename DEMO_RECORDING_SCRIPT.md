# Ontogeny Demo Recording Script

**Duration:** 3-5 minutes
**Setup:** Desktop UI running, Demo tab open, screen recording active

---

## Opening (0:00 - 0:20)

**Narration:**
"This is Ontogeny, a proto-AGI cognitive agent that autonomously acquires knowledge, reasons about evidence, and recursively improves its own capabilities. Today I'll walk through a demo of its integrated cognitive workflow."

**Action:** Show the full desktop UI with sidebar visible. Point out the clean, native-AI workstation aesthetic.

---

## Starting the Demo (0:20 - 0:40)

**Narration:**
"Let me start Demo Mode. This runs a guided walkthrough using controlled demonstration fixtures — no live model calls, no network requests, just the real system schemas with realistic data."

**Action:**
1. Click the **Demo** tab in the sidebar
2. Click **Start Demo**
3. Point out the progress bar and step list

---

## Goal & Planning (0:40 - 1:10)

**Narration:**
"Step 1: Ontogeny receives a research objective — investigate neuromorphic computing for energy-efficient AI inference. Step 2: The planning system generates a 6-step research plan with dependencies and confidence targets."

**Action:** Show the Demo panel with steps 1-2 completed. Point out the plan structure.

**Click Next Step** to advance through steps 1-2.

---

## Knowledge Acquisition (1:10 - 2:00)

**Narration:**
"Step 3: The Knowledge Acquisition System retrieves evidence from multiple sources — ArXiv papers, Semantic Scholar citations, HuggingFace datasets. Each source is scored for quality and relevance."

**Action:**
1. Advance to step 3
2. Scroll down to show the **Evidence Acquired** panel
3. Point out the source names, confidence scores, and summaries
4. Show that 4 documents were retrieved with confidence scores above 85%

---

## Evidence Validation & Memory (2:00 - 2:30)

**Narration:**
"Step 4: Evidence is validated — source quality scorer rates all sources above 0.85 confidence. No contradictions found. Step 5: Key findings are written to persistent memory. The knowledge graph now has 8 concepts and 7 relations."

**Action:**
1. Advance to steps 4-5
2. Show the **Knowledge Graph** panel with concept nodes
3. Point out the node count and edge count

---

## Reflection (2:30 - 3:00)

**Narration:**
"Step 6: Ontogeny reflects on the research. It identifies what worked — multi-source triangulation — and what's missing — practical deployment cost data. The self-model is updated with a new strength and a new blind spot."

**Action:**
1. Advance to step 6
2. Show the **Reflection** panel
3. Point out the insight, gap, and lesson fields

---

## Maldoror Proposal (3:00 - 3:40)

**Narration:**
"Step 7: Maldoror, the self-modification agent, proposes an improvement — add source-category-aware scheduling for hardware research topics. This is a dry-run proposal: syntax validated, import-safe, sandbox-tested, but not applied. The diff preview shows exactly what would change."

**Action:**
1. Advance to step 7
2. Show the **Maldoror Proposal** panel
3. Point out the validation badges (syntax valid, import safe, sandbox passed)
4. Show the dry-run diff

---

## Summary & Closing (3:40 - 4:00)

**Narration:**
"Step 8: Demo complete. In this session, Ontogeny analyzed 23 sources, added 4 concepts to the knowledge graph, wrote 5 memories, and proposed 1 improvement — all without modifying production source code. This is the core cognitive workflow: Goal, Planning, Knowledge Acquisition, Evidence, Memory, Reasoning, Reflection, and Safe Recursive Improvement."

**Action:**
1. Show the **Session Summary** panel with final metrics
2. Click **Reset** to show the demo can be cleanly restarted
3. Show the sidebar with all tabs visible

---

## Optional: Quick Tab Tour (4:00 - 4:30)

**Narration:**
"Beyond the demo, Ontogeny has 11 panels covering the full cognitive stack — activity timeline, cognitive state, memory explorer, goal tree, knowledge graph, acquisition, Maldoror, production metrics, Blender sandbox, MuJoCo robotics, and settings."

**Action:** Quickly click through 2-3 tabs (Activity, Maldoror, MuJoCo) to show the breadth of the interface.

---

## Closing (4:30 - 4:45)

**Narration:**
"Ontogeny is open source under AGPL-3.0. The demo used controlled fixtures, but the full system runs autonomously with Ollama for LLM routing, 30 acquisition engines, and MuJoCo for robotics simulation. Thank you."

**Action:** End on the main UI view.

---

## Recording Notes

- Use 1920x1080 or higher resolution
- Record at 30fps minimum
- Use a clean desktop background
- Close unnecessary applications
- Disable notifications during recording
- Use the dark theme for best visual contrast
- Narrate clearly and at a moderate pace
- Pause briefly between steps to let viewers absorb the information
